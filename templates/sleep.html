Sleep Hygiene Best Practices

• Light Isolation

Use blackout curtains and avoid blue screen exposure for 45 minutes before sleep. Blue light suppresses melatonin release.

• Dark, Cool Room

Set your room temperature to a cool setting (around 18-22 degrees Celsius). Lower temperatures mimic the body's natural sleep cycle transitions.

• Consistent Timings

Go to sleep and wake up at the exact same times every day. Consistency anchors your body's circadian clock for quality rest.
